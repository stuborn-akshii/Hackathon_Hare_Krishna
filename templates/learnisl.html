Learn Indian Sign Language

Here are some videos to learn some gestures from the Indian Sign Language. These videos have been sourced from the Indian Sign Language Dictionary 3rd edition developed by the Indian Sign Language Research and Training Centre (ISLRTC), Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD), Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Govt. of India.

Video Tutorials

Afternoon

To sign "afternoon," start by extending your non-dominant hand, palm facing upward. Then, with your dominant hand, form a flat handshape with fingers slightly apart. Gently tap the center of your non-dominant palm a couple of times using the flat handshape. This tapping motion represents the idea of the sun being high in the sky during the afternoon hours. While performing this gesture, maintain a neutral facial expression or a subtle indication of relaxation. The sign for "afternoon" helps to communicate the specific time of day, particularly the period following the morning and preceding the evening.

Allergy

To sign "allergy," Start with both hands open and palms facing your body, fingers relaxed. Movement: Bring both hands up to your face, near your nose, with palms facing inward. While keeping your hands in this position, move them outward and slightly away from your face, as if depicting the dispersion of particles or discomfort. Facial expression: Maintain a concerned or uncomfortable facial expression, emphasizing the discomfort associated with an allergic reaction.

Blood

In Indian Sign Language (ISL), the sign for "blood" is performed using a specific handshape and movement. Here's a description of the sign for "blood" in ISL: Start with your non-dominant hand open, palm facing upward. With your dominant hand, form a fist with the index finger extended, pointing downward. Place the tip of your extended index finger on the back of your non-dominant hand, near the wrist area. While maintaining the finger position, move your dominant hand slightly forward and backward, tapping or brushing the back of your non-dominant hand with the tip of your index finger. Maintain a neutral expression, as facial expressions are not a significant component of this sign.

Doctor

To perform the sign for "doctor" in Indian Sign Language (ISL), you can follow these steps: Begin with your dominant hand in a closed fist, with the thumb resting on top of the fingers. Bring your dominant hand close to your non-dominant forearm, near the elbow. Then, move your dominant hand in a circular motion, as if you are examining or checking the arm. Maintain a professional and focused facial expression to convey the seriousness and expertise associated with the role of a doctor.

Today

To sign "today" in Indian Sign Language (ISL), follow these steps: Begin with both hands in a relaxed, open position, with palms facing inward. Touch the thumb of your dominant hand to your chin, then move it forward and slightly downward in a smooth arc, ending with your palm facing upward. While performing the sign, maintain a neutral facial expression or a subtle smile to convey the presentness and positivity of "today." The sign is performed in a fluid motion, emphasizing the connection between the present moment and the concept of "today."

Winter

In Indian Sign Language (ISL), the sign for "winter" captures the essence of the cold and chilly season. Here's a description of the sign for "winter" in ISL:

To sign "winter" in ISL,Start with both hands in a relaxed, open position, with fingers slightly spread apart. Bring both hands up to your chest level, with palms facing each other. Flutter your fingers rapidly, simulating falling snowflakes. This gesture symbolizes the cold, swirling motion of snowflakes drifting down during winter. To enhance the meaning, wear a slightly serious or chilly expression, mimicking the feeling of coldness associated with winter.
